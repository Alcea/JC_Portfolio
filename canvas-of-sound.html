Canvas of Sound

Imagine the ability to see sound in the form of colors, spatially oriented in front of you. This simultaneous occurring sensation is called synesthesia. According to the American Psychological Association, 1 in 2000 people actually have this realistic superpower. Canvas of Sound focuses on sensory based immersion specifically what the person wearing the virtual reality headset is seeing and hearing. This VR environment was a course project was created using Unity by myself and two other classmates with the project theme of "going beyond" to evoke a sense of wonder, empathy and provide an interesting experience that someone may normally not be able to experience. My role in the project was the project leader, sound director and main programmer.

Starting with the theme that was given, our group did an affinity diagramming exercise that resulted in three categories: simulation, psychological, and sightseeing. Synesthesia came as an after-thought while suggesting topics that would fall under all three categories. As the project leader, I organized and directed the flow of work based on the strengths of the team members to meet milestone deadlines, as well as promoted discussion and made the finalization of each milestone hand-in. As the main programmer, I worked with the flow of the simulation, interactivity of the objects, and aided in the environment design. The project went through three major iterations of both narrative and environment elements to create the final product. The scope was limited to one synesthetic affect instead of the original three that we wanted to create for the sake of time. A lot of consideration was put into movement design in order to decrease the sense of motion sickness. As the sound director, I designed the sequence of the audio as well as what type of audio would appear upon interacting with objects.
